Grammar session
  1. I choose 3 idioms for daily usage with the examples.
    1. “I am game”
      Meaning:
      I am willing to try or participate in something (even if it is new or challenging).
      Usage: Informal; used to show agreement to do something.
      Examples:
      “Do you want to try hiking tomorrow?” — “Sure, I’m game.”
      Everyone was tired, but she was still game for another round of practice.

    2. “Every dog has his day”
      Meaning:
      Everyone will have a moment of success or good luck at some point.
      Usage: Used to encourage someone or to describe a surprising
      success. Examples:
      He finally won the competition after many attempts — every dog has his day.
      Don’t feel discouraged; every dog has his day.

    3. “I beg to differ”
      Meaning:
      A polite way to say you disagree with someone.
      Usage: Formal or polite disagreement in discussions.
      Examples:
      “This plan will not work.” — “I beg to differ; I think it has great potential.”
      I beg to differ with your opinion about the project timeline.

Grammar session
  1. I choose 3 idioms for daily usage with the examples.
    1. “Break the ice”
      Meaning:
      To start a conversation in a social situation where people feel shy, awkward, or don’t know each other.
      Usage: Used when talking about making people feel comfortable at the beginning of a meeting, class, date, or event.
      Examples:
      The teacher told a joke to break the ice on the first day of class.
      I asked her about her hometown to break the ice.
      Playing games can help break the ice in a workshop.

    2. “Piece of cake”
      Meaning:
      Something very easy to do.
      Usage: Used to describe tasks, tests, jobs, or activities that are not difficult.
      Examples:
      The English quiz was a piece of cake.
      Don’t worry about the interview — it will be a piece of cake for you.
      Cooking noodles is a piece of cake.

    3. “Call it a day”
      Meaning:
      To stop working on something for the day because you are tired or finished.
      Usage: Common in work, study, or projects.
      Examples:
      We finished the report, so let’s call it a day.
      I’m too tired to continue studying — I’ll call it a day.
      After cleaning the house, she called it a day.
